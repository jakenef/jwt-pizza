# Incident: 2026-04-03 13:43-14:23

## Summary

Between 13:43 and 14:23 on 2026-04-03, users experienced a significantly increased rate of pizza purchase failures. The issue was caused by the CS 329 pizza factory service returning HTTP 500 errors due to an active chaos monkey. This resulted in intermittent spikes of failed purchase attempts, effectively doubling the normal error rate during affected periods.

A bug in alerting configuration allowed the issue to go undetected automatically, as error spikes lasted only one minute at a time and never met the two-minute threshold required to trigger an alert. The issue was manually discovered and mitigated by disabling the chaos monkey. This was a medium severity incident affecting all users attempting to purchase pizza during the impact window.

Approximately 14 pizza purchases were lost due to this incident.

## Detection

This incident was not detected by automated alerting systems. Instead, it was discovered manually at 14:00 by Jacob Nef, who was on call and investigating logs out of curiosity.

Grafana showed periodic spikes in error rates, but the configured alert required two consecutive minutes of elevated errors. Since the error rate alternated between normal and elevated every minute, the alert never triggered.

To improve time-to-detection, alerting should be updated to:

- Trigger on shorter spikes (e.g., 1-minute sustained anomalies)
- Use rolling averages instead of strict consecutive thresholds

This improvement should be implemented by the monitoring/infra team to ensure intermittent failures are detected more reliably.

## Impact

For 40 minutes between 13:43 and 14:23 UTC on 04/03/26, users attempting to purchase pizza experienced a high likelihood of failure.

This incident affected all users making purchase attempts during that time window, with approximately 14 failed transactions recorded.

No support tickets were reported, but user impact was measurable through failed purchase attempts.

## Response

After noticing unusual behavior at 14:00 UTC, Jacob Nef began investigating logs without having received any alerts.

Once the root cause was identified at 14:20 UTC, action was taken immediately using a control link provided in the error responses. The chaos monkey was disabled within minutes, resolving the issue by 14:23 UTC.

There were no major delays in response once the issue was identified, but detection was delayed due to ineffective alerting.

## Root cause

The root cause was an active chaos monkey in the CS 329 pizza factory service causing intermittent HTTP 500 errors.

This was compounded by an alerting configuration that required two consecutive minutes of elevated error rates, which failed to detect alternating one-minute spikes.

## Resolution

The incident was resolved by disabling the chaos monkey via a control link provided in the JWT pizza factory error responses.

After disabling the chaos monkey, error rates immediately returned to baseline levels. The system was monitored for several minutes to confirm stability and ensure no further disruptions.

Time to mitigation could be improved by:

- Ensuring faster automated detection
- Providing clearer visibility into chaos experiments in production systems

## Prevention

This class of incident could recur if:

- Chaos experiments are enabled without proper visibility or safeguards
- Alerting systems fail to detect intermittent failures

Future mitigation should include:

- Better visibility and control over chaos monkey activity
- Improved alerting sensitivity to intermittent spikes
- Safeguards preventing chaos testing in production without explicit authorization
